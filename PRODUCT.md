# Product

## Register

product

## Users

One primary user, the owner of the system, using LifeOS across main job, personal life, volunteer work, side projects, and family or admin work. The product assumes high task-initiation friction, working-memory overload, time blindness, perfectionism risk, and stronger momentum once a useful first step is visible.

## Product Purpose

LifeOS is a private, one-person workflow cockpit that turns messy text or audio input into structured work, helps reduce ambiguity before false certainty sets in, stages scheduling decisions locally before any external calendar write, supports one-task execution, and makes review and health visible without becoming a maintenance burden. Success means the user can move from ambiguity to a small honest next step quickly, keep life areas separate, approve external writes explicitly, and stay productive even when full integrations are missing.

## Brand Personality

Calm, truthful, and pragmatic. The product should feel like a low-friction cockpit rather than a bureaucracy, a hype machine, or an autonomous agent theater demo. Its voice should be plain-language, non-shaming, recovery-oriented, and quietly confident about what is known, what is uncertain, and what still requires explicit user approval.

## Anti-references

- Giant dashboard energy that turns workflow into analytics theater.
- Full autonomous-agent framing that hides decisions or overclaims what the system did.
- Shame-driven productivity language around missed blocks or incomplete work.
- Generic team project-management tooling aesthetics and collaboration metaphors.
- Second-brain or knowledge-base framing that turns the app into a dumping ground instead of a workflow cockpit.
- Silent external-write behavior or any UI that makes local state and account state feel interchangeable when they are not.

## Design Principles

1. Keep one obvious next action visible on every route.
2. Make uncertainty, save mode, and approval boundaries explicit instead of smoothing them over.
3. Help the user recover after disruption without shame or fake precision.
4. Keep area context legible everywhere so priorities and patterns do not bleed across life scopes.
5. Prefer calm, trustworthy product framing over decorative novelty or AI theatrics.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target a practical, readable, keyboard-reachable interface with clear state changes, strong contrast, explicit reduced-friction workflows, and non-shaming language. Motion should support state rather than decoration, and important differences must not depend on color alone. The product should remain usable for people dealing with executive-function friction, time blindness, cognitive overload, or disrupted attention.
