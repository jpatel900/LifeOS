03 — Three directions

Pick how far to go

Each keeps capture, areas, AI sorting, drafts and history. They differ in how much they reveal at rest.

OPTION 01 · RADICAL REDUCTION

Just capture

The screen is a blank page and a caret. Type, hit ⌘↵, it's saved. Area, drafts and recent history live one keystroke away behind / and the bottom drawer — never shown until you reach for them.

Keeps — full save / save&organize, area tagging, drafts, history.
Trade-off — best for people who already trust the flow; less self-explanatory for newcomers.

OPTION 02 · BALANCED

Capture & quiet context

One composer, one primary Save with the alternate folded into a split menu. A single slim rail on the right replaces the eight cards: area, a live count of what's waiting, and the last three captures. Two zones instead of eight.

Keeps — every control visible, just grouped and de-ranked.
Trade-off — the safest move; least surprising for current users.

OPTION 03 · KEYBOARD COCKPIT

Flow as a command line

The six stages become a thin progress rail you move along. Capture is a calm composer over a single mono command bar — every action is a key, every key is shown. Powerful without looking busy: low contrast does the quieting.

Keeps — the whole pipeline, surfaced as one legible spine.
Trade-off — most opinionated; rewards repeat use over first impression.
